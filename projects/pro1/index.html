Intelligent Transportation Network Driven by Digital Twin

This project was funded by Wuhan Traffic Management Bureau.

This research attempts to use the burgeoning big data technologies to investigate the urban transportation system based on digital twins. It solves the disadvantages of conventional approaches, such as limited data support and poor analytical precision, and speeds the integration of essential technologies, such as artificial intelligence, with transportation-specific knowledge.

The purpose of this project is mainly the following three points. Initially, the value of traffic data may be increased by fusing and mining vast amount of data. Secondly, construct a digital twin system to facilitate the automated development of complicated road networks. In the end, accurate repair and development of the visual output of the urban transportation system.

In this project, my job includes

  • Building up a multi-modal transportation road network in the simulation software SUMO, including the basic network, bus stops, parking lots, and traffic lights.
  • Verifying the authenticity of the simulation, such as comparing the bus lines or the passing time of each phase of the traffic lights between reality and simulation.
